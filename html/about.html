About Nairobi National Park

Location

Nairobi National Park is located just 7 kilometers south of Nairobi, the capital city of Kenya. Despite its proximity to the urban area, the park is renowned for its diverse wildlife and natural beauty.

Overview

Nairobi National Park is a unique wildlife reserve that offers a quintessential African safari experience. Established in 1946, the park spans approximately 117 square kilometers and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna.

Wildlife

Visitors to the park can witness the "Big Five" (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros) along with other iconic African animals such as giraffes, zebras, cheetahs, and numerous species of antelope. The park is also a haven for birdwatchers, boasting over 400 bird species.

Scenic Landscapes

The park features a diverse landscape, including open grass plains, acacia woodlands, and riverine forests. The backdrop of the city skyline against the wildlife creates a unique juxtaposition, making it a popular destination for photographers.

Activities

Safari Drives

Guided safari drives offer visitors the chance to explore the park's vast landscapes and observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

Picnic Sites

Enjoy a relaxing day at designated picnic spots within the park, providing a unique setting to savor the beauty of nature.

Animal Orphanage

The Nairobi Animal Orphanage within the park is a rehabilitation center for orphaned or injured animals, providing educational opportunities for visitors.

Visitor Facilities

Visitor Center

The park's visitor center offers information, maps, and educational resources about the wildlife and conservation efforts.

Souvenir Shops

Find unique mementos, crafts, and souvenirs at the shops within the park.

Restaurants

Enjoy local and international cuisine at the on-site restaurants.

Conservation Efforts

Nairobi National Park plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation and environmental education. The park collaborates with various conservation organizations to protect endangered species and promote sustainable practices.

Accessibility

The park is easily accessible by road from Nairobi city center, and transportation options, including guided tours, are available for visitors.
